Visual Art Analysis

Spoliarium by Juan Luna-

The scenario at a Roman amphitheater where the dead bodies of two gladiators are being dragged toward the Spoliarium is shown in Juan Lunas artwork Spoliarium. The Roman Colosseums basement was known as the Spoliarium, where dead gladiators were stripped of their armor and weapons and subsequently readied for burial. Scavengers are seen on the left side of the artwork, eyeing the deceased mens valuables. A woman is sitting on the ground to the right, lamenting the loss of a loved one.

Pasyong Bayan by Imelda Cajipe-Endaya-

Imelda Cajipe-Endaya is one of the famous Contemporary Filipino Artist who also became a main figure for Filipino feminist and national liberation movements in Philippine Arts. Her artwork "Pasyong Bayan" (A Nation’s Passion) showed the adversities of women, as well as a protest of human rights against the dictatorship of Ferdinand Marcos. I personally believe that her artworks aim to give awareness and also a wake up call at the time during the martial law of how important human rights are and how without it, people would suffer and their freedom and rights will be violated. It is also to encourage and promote female artists as part of their mission as a group. It also addresses the needs of the community.

Amongst the Lost People by Annie Cabigting-

"Amongst the Lost People" is an artwork created by Annie Cabigting a Filipino Asian Modern and Contemporary painter in the year 2015. The artwork depicts a woman examining a painting that represents the 8th circle of hell, based on the famous novel "The Inferno" by Dante Alighieri. Personally, this painting can serve as a reminder for us to unwind from the tiring work from our daily lives. Remind us to always rethink our choices and actions in life to avoid being sinned against and sent to hell. Socially, politically, and spiritually, we must be kind and never be opportunistic toward fragile people.

Isang Sakong Bigas Ni Atlas by Elmer Misa Borlongan-

Elmer Misa Borlongan (born January 7, 1967) is a prominent contemporary Filipino painter best known for his distinctive use of figurative expressionism. He rose to prominence as a recipient of the Cultural Center of the Philippines Thirteen Artist Awards in 1994. The art is contains a man who lift or carried a sack of rice and his clothes are veiled in his head. His face looks tired and hungry. Based on how his body is built, he is a diligent man and he works hard for his family. The background saying that no one can help you but yourself. It makes me motivated to the point that I'll try to do my best and pursue what I want without the help of others.

Yellow Confeti by Ben Cabrera-

Benedicto Reyes Cabrera, better known as "BenCab", is a Filipino painter and was awarded National Artist of the Philippines for Visual Arts in 2006. He has been noted as "arguably the best-selling painter of his generation of Filipino artists. His artwork (Yellow Confetti) captures the humanistic face of a period caught in a flux, articulating the passion, energy and desire for economic stability and social justice; as well as the underlying fabric and foundation of contemporary Philippines, an epoch of political history within the last thirty years.

Greyhounds by Ronald Ventura-

Ronald Ventura, who was born in Manila, Philippines in 1973, graduated from the University of Sto. Tomas in 1993 His dynamic blending of realism, cartoons, and graffiti has made him a well-known modern Filipino artist. To depict scenes of utter chaos, Ventura draws inspiration from popular comic book characters, science fiction, Western history, Asian mythology, and Catholicism. The words "THE BATTLE OF THE CENTURY" are inscribed underneath the ravaging horses, robot, detached muscles, and shadowy individuals, with the word "century" barely discernible.His works of art conceal a lovely message about how society must develop.

Song Art Analysis

Spoliarium by Eraserheads-

Spoliarium – It is a popular song by the band "Eraserheads," which released in 1997 with the album "Sticker Happy." Because of the song's rumor-related content and hidden message, it attracted controversy when it was first released. They claim that Pepsi Paloma, a well-known actress from the 1980s, was involved. The article claims that Ricardo "Richie D'Horsie" Reyes, Joey de Leon, and Vic Sotto were the three hosts who sexually abused Pepsi Paloma. When "Tito Sotto" got involved and compelled Paloma to drop her accusations against the hosts, the situation heated up. Pepsi Paloma committed suicide in 1985, at the age of 18. Decades passed, Ely Buendia, front man of E-heads, revealed the true meaning of the song. He said that there’s no such connections as people believed. It is all about getting pissed drank nothing more. He added that this myth of the song will continue even he talks about it.

Pinoy Ako by Orange Lemon-

Pinoy Ako – It was released in 2005, composed by Jonathan Manalo and sung by the pop-rock band Orange and Lemons. It serves as Pinoy Big Brother's main theme song and the foundation for a large portion of the musical scoring used in the reality series. The major motto of "Pinoy Ako" is "Pagpapakatotoo”. The song also aims to inspire Filipinos to be proud of and true to themselves despite their origins, where they live, and the difficulties they encounter. It demonstrates how a Filipino can bravely represent the Philippines to the world by being true to who they are and not living up to others' expectations. As stated by Clem Castro, the band's goal has always been to create "timeless" music. The music they have created, with its memorable lyrics that preserve their music as memories, is for future generations to hear.

Upuan by Gloc 9-

Upuan – It is the first single of Gloc 9 from his fourth album Matrikula. The song features Jeazell Grutas, vocalist of the band Zelle. It was released on June 29, 2009. The song received numerous awards and most of them are from the 23rd Music Awards. It is a message from politics especially that owns a prestigious chair. It also discusses the Philippines' situation in the face of corruption, political dynasty, and poverty. The lyrics freely convey the harsh reality and the authorities' greed for power, are what made it so potent. Gloc 9 concluded the song with a well-known Filipino proverb that included a message for the offended party.

Isang Sakong Bigas Ni Atlas by Elmer Misa Borlongan-

DNDN – It is a song released in 2020. The lyrics is all about the artists story of life experiences. It says that time is gold so don’t let it waste to nothing. Instead use it to gain experience to reach the goal and succession in life. He also conveys that he wants a fresh environment to relieve the stress of society. This song is a reflection of what is the personality and the life that the artist aspire of.

Revolving around the story of a certain overseas Filipino worker, the song narrates how he has to make sacrifices for being away from his family, only for an unexpected twist to happen when it was his time to return.
